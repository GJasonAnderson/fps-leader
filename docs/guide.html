Week 3

Life / Intro Questions (Choose one or two)

  • What are you excited about doing this month? (Bring up SKO)
  • What does the cross symbolize in your life?
  • What are some things that you hope and wish for in your life?

Connection Question

  • Have you ever had a time where you felt hopeless? How did you get yourself out of that?

Sermon in a Sentence

  • Only Jesus can to bring the dead back to life.

Scripture

  • John 11:21-27 - "Lord," Martha said to Jesus, "if you had been here, my brother would not have died. But I know that even now God will give you whatever you ask." Jesus said to her, "Your brother will rise again." Martha answered, "I know he will rise again in the resurrection at the last day." Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die; and whoever lives by believing in me will never die. Do you believe this?" "Yes, Lord," she replied, "I believe that you are the Messiah, the Son of God, who is to come into the world."
  • What is Jesus saying in these scriptures?

Discussion Questions

  • If comfortable, what are some things in your life that make you feel broken or lost?
  • Why do you think that we as humans believe we're too "broken" to be loved by God?
  • Has there ever been a time in your life where you felt that Jesus "resurrected" something for you like He did with Lazarus and his family? It doesn't have to be someone being raised from the dead but maybe Jesus brought you hope, love, or helped you through hard times.

Life Application

We want students walking away with 1 or 2 things they are going to do SOON to apply what they discussed.

  • How do you think you can find hope in hopeless situations in the next week?
  • Ask students who they will tell about how Jesus change their lives by "resurrecting" them?

Take It Out

  • Close small group out with prayer requests.
  • After prayer, be sure to remind them of SKO, Movement and THE WEEKEND!
